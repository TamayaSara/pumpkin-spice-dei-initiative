ERG

ERG stands for Employee Resource Groups. Each employee-led group represents a community, an identity, and a support system for our underrepresented groups. Attend our events, create lasting connections, and feel more at home in The Alpaca Company by attending these events for our groups.

  1. 1. Register on this website using your employee ID and log in to join any ERGs you are interested in, or even start one of your own.
  2. 2. Stay posted with our events through our upcoming events calendar.
  3. 3. For more information, reach out to the senior representative for the resource group(s) you want to join or are already a part of.

Our Employee Resource Groups

SkinTECH

People of all ethnicities and races are given the platform to share and relate to one another their difficulties in the workplace. It will open up a comfortable space for our marginalized ethnic groups to discuss microaggressions and opportunities where we can include cultural expression into our work. Here, we share what’s going well within the company’s efforts of inclusion and fixes we need to see in the workplace to accommodate our diverse staff.

WomenInTech

WomanInTech is a place where women can safely feel able to enhance their work skills, engage in supportive talks, and grow their confidence in the workplace. The space gives a safe place to discuss women-centered issues, including how to protect their status during pregnancy and maternity leave, taking leadership, and asking for promotions. This ERG pairs members with a mentor or peer to support them throughout their Alpaca Company career.

DisabilityInTech

People with disabilities are able to have the visibility they need within the workplace in DisabilityInTech. We discuss issues within the company or our personal lives that may impact us, including discrimination, the feeling of otherness, and lack of accessibility. We help others with similar disabilities seek accommodation with proper form in the workplace. The group will consistently search for feedback to improve the next meeting in the hope that more people to engage in the conversation. We use our voice to give a better outline on a fairer environment for people with disabilities.
